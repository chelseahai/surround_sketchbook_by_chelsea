Research Questions

Exploring fundamental inquiries that drive our research and design process

The Foundation

What happens when your garment options stops being static and starts listening—to the day, to the city, but mostly to you? These questions explore garments as living systems: shaping comfort through data, and rethinking fashion beyond consumption, and define meanings as a layer between the world and you.

01
How can computational design mediate the relationship between environmental conditions and the body through adaptive garments?
This question explores the role of algorithmic logic in shaping garments that respond dynamically to changing climates and bodily states. It raises the idea of clothing as an interface—an active mediator rather than a passive layer—blurring the line between fashion, technology, and environmental adaptation.
02
Can real-time environmental and bodily data inform decision-making processes in dressing and movement planning?
Here, the focus shifts to temporal immediacy: what happens when our clothes—and the systems behind them—respond not to forecasts but to lived, moment-to-moment conditions? It questions whether dressing could evolve from a static choice to a dynamic negotiation guided by sensory and contextual feedback loops.
03
What ethical questions arise when personal and geospatial data are used to inform intimate daily practices?
Embedding adaptive logic into fashion introduces surveillance-adjacent concerns: whose data is processed, who owns it, and how visible should it be? This question foregrounds the tension between personalization and privacy when computational systems enter the realm of intimate, bodily experience.
04
How might adaptive wearables contribute to resilience in increasingly volatile urban environments shaped by climate change?
This inquiry situates adaptive garments within the larger framework of climate resilience, asking whether clothing can become infrastructure. It reflects on fashion not as ornament but as a survival strategy—a personal-scale response to systemic environmental volatility.
05
Could this framework challenge fast fashion by prioritizing adaptive, multi-scenario garments over static consumption?
This question critiques the dominant cycles of fashion by introducing adaptability as a counter-narrative to disposability. It asks whether garments that morph across contexts could disrupt trend-driven consumption and propose an alternative model for sustainable luxury.
