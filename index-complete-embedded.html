Research Questions

Q1
How can we design a layer that connects body and environment?
This question explores the fundamental relationship between our physical selves and the spaces we inhabit, seeking to understand how design can mediate and enhance this connection through thoughtful intervention and technological augmentation.
Q2
What are some ways that wearable technology can sense internal body awareness to be aware of the body?
Investigating biometric sensors, physiological monitoring, and embodied interfaces that can capture and interpret internal bodily states.
Q3
What are some ways that the body can be aware of the environment?
Exploring environmental sensing, context awareness, and systems that can inform the body about surrounding conditions and changes.
Q4
How could the designed layer respond to the world around them?
Examining adaptive responses, reactive materials, and dynamic interfaces that can change based on environmental conditions and user needs.
Q5
How could the designed layer be of the world?
Considering integration, seamless interaction, and how the designed intervention becomes part of the natural ecosystem rather than separate from it.
Q6
How could the designed layer be responsive to the body?
Investigating body-responsive technologies, adaptive fit, and systems that can respond to physical changes, movements, and bodily needs.

The Bedroom Metaphor

The bedroom is the first space of the day, the moment between rest and movement, private and public. In Surround, this space becomes a metaphor for tuning into the self before stepping into the city. Here, the system asks questions of the body: How ready are you? Do you feel warmth or stillness? Are you calm, or charged with energy?

Through simple, sensory interactions—adjusting a silhouette, pressing to sense warmth, moving to gauge mental energy, the bedroom interface translates feelings into data. This is where subjective states become design input, shaping how the layer between you and the world will respond. The bedroom is not just a starting point; it is an act of authorship, letting you design your own comfort and agency before the environment begins to speak.

Function

Captures the body's internal state—readiness, warmth, energy, and visibility intent—through interactive gestures like cursor movement, press-and-hold, and proximity.

Narrative

The mirror is about self-awareness before engagement. It asks: How do you feel in this moment? By translating these subtle internal signals into data, the system allows the user to co-author their experience for the day. It's an intimate starting point—designing from the inside out.

Displays environmental conditions and route planning through live geospatial data (Mapbox + weather APIs). Visual overlays reveal sunlight intensity, wind flow, and crowd density.

Narrative

The map is the bridge between internal awareness and external reality. It reads the city like a body—where is it warm, where is it crowded, where can you find solitude? This interface transforms urban navigation from efficiency-focused routing into sensory-aware wayfinding, designed for comfort and intentionality.
