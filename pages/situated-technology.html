Situated Technology

Exploring technology embedded in context and place

The Context

Technology is never neutral—it exists within specific contexts, shaped by the environments, cultures, and bodies it serves. Situated technology recognizes that the most meaningful innovations emerge from deep understanding of place, context, and human experience. This approach challenges us to think beyond universal solutions and instead create technologies that are responsive to their specific situations.

What is your orientation to the Computational Design Practices theme?

Context
Environmental Responsiveness
Technologies that adapt to their environmental context—responding to climate, geography, and local conditions. These systems learn from their surroundings and adjust their behavior accordingly, creating more resilient and appropriate solutions.
Culture
Cultural Integration
Technologies that respect and integrate with local cultural practices, values, and traditions. This approach ensures that innovations enhance rather than disrupt existing social structures and ways of knowing.
Body
Embodied Interaction
Technologies that understand and respond to the body's specific needs, movements, and limitations. These systems create more intuitive and accessible interactions that feel natural and supportive.
Place
Place-Based Design
Technologies that emerge from and serve specific places, understanding the unique characteristics, histories, and needs of particular locations and communities.
Time
Temporal Adaptation
Technologies that respond to temporal rhythms—daily cycles, seasonal changes, and long-term transformations. These systems understand that context is not static but constantly evolving.
Scale
Multi-Scalar Systems
Technologies that operate across multiple scales—from the intimate scale of the body to the global scale of environmental systems—creating connections between local and global contexts.
