Methods

Exploring the methodologies and approaches

The Approach

Our research methodology combines computational design, material experimentation, and human-centered inquiry to develop adaptive systems that respond to environmental and bodily contexts. These methods bridge traditional craft practices with emerging technologies, creating new possibilities for responsive, sustainable design.

Research
Computational Design
Using algorithmic processes and parametric modeling to generate responsive forms and systems. This approach enables the creation of complex geometries and adaptive behaviors that respond to environmental and user inputs.
Material
Smart Materials
Experimenting with materials that change properties in response to environmental stimuli—temperature, humidity, light, or mechanical stress. These materials become active components in responsive systems.
Sensing
Environmental Sensing
Integrating sensors to monitor environmental conditions and user states, creating feedback loops that inform adaptive responses. This method enables real-time interaction between systems and their contexts.
Prototyping
Iterative Development
Rapid prototyping and testing cycles that allow for continuous refinement of responsive systems. This approach emphasizes learning through making and user feedback.
Integration
System Integration
Combining computational systems, material properties, and environmental data to create cohesive adaptive experiences. This method ensures that all components work together harmoniously.
Evaluation
Performance Assessment
Evaluating the effectiveness of adaptive systems through user studies, environmental testing, and performance metrics. This method ensures that designs meet their intended goals and user needs.

Computational Design Experiments

Our experimental work explores the boundaries of computational design, material intelligence, and environmental responsiveness. These experiments serve as both research probes and practical demonstrations of adaptive systems in action.

Experiment 1
Responsive Textile Systems
Developing textiles that respond to environmental conditions through embedded sensors and actuators. These experiments explore how fabric can become an active interface between the body and its surroundings.
Experiment 2
Computational Pattern Generation
Using algorithmic processes to generate adaptive patterns that respond to environmental data. These experiments bridge digital design and physical fabrication to create dynamic, responsive surfaces.
Experiment 3
Environmental Data Visualization
Transforming environmental data into visual and tactile experiences through wearable interfaces. These experiments explore how data can become embodied and meaningful in everyday contexts.
Experiment 4
Adaptive Form Generation
Creating forms that evolve and adapt based on environmental inputs and user interactions. These experiments push the boundaries of what's possible with computational design and responsive materials.
Experiment 5
Material Intelligence
Exploring materials that exhibit intelligent behaviors—changing properties, responding to stimuli, and adapting to their environment. These experiments investigate the potential for materials to become active participants in design.
Experiment 6
Human-Environment Interaction
Designing systems that facilitate meaningful interaction between humans and their environments. These experiments explore how technology can enhance our relationship with the world around us.
