Methods and Computational Design Experiments

Exploring the methodologies and approaches

The Approach

This project combines computational design methods, real-time environmental data integration, and interactive web-based experiences. The software layer includes JavaScript frameworks (Mapbox GL, D3.js, GSAP) for geospatial visualization and motion design, along with HTML/CSS for UI structure and Three.js experiments for parametric effects. Data practices focus on pulling live environmental and mobility data from APIs, translating them into garment behavior logic.

Theoretical Framework

The theoretical approach builds on cybernetic systems, adaptive design, and body–environment mediation, paired with critical design discourse on sustainability and urban resilience. Aesthetically, the project draws from fashion editorial language merged with data-driven visualization to create a narrative that feels cinematic yet interactive.

Methodological Approach

Both qualitative and quantitative methods inform the process: Qualitative through speculative design framing, narrative prototyping, and visual language exploration. Quantitative through environmental dataset analysis, algorithmic garment simulations, and user-interaction metrics.

Critical Components

The most critical components are computational and aesthetic, as the project relies on real-time data and UI logic to shape a coherent visual and experiential identity. Physical and material aspects play a conceptual role, suggesting tactile futures through rendered garments rather than full prototypes for now. Written components remain essential for framing—providing context, narrative, and ethical positioning.

Technology Stack

The project leverages a combination of modern web technologies to create interactive, data-driven experiences that bridge the digital and physical realms.

Mapbox GL
Geospatial visualization and mapping for environmental data representation and location-based garment behavior.
D3.js
Data visualization and dynamic charting for environmental metrics and garment performance analytics.
GSAP
Motion design and animation for creating fluid, cinematic transitions and responsive garment movements.
Three.js
3D parametric effects and spatial visualization for garment form generation and environmental modeling.
HTML/CSS
UI structure and styling for creating intuitive, responsive interfaces that adapt to user interactions.
API Integration
Real-time environmental and mobility data feeds that inform garment behavior and adaptive responses.

Narrative Building

The Bedroom Metaphor

The bedroom is the first space of the day, the moment between rest and movement, private and public. In Surround, this space becomes a metaphor for tuning into the self before stepping into the city. Here, the system asks questions of the body: How ready are you? Do you feel warmth or stillness? Are you calm, or charged with energy?

Through simple, sensory interactions—adjusting a silhouette, pressing to sense warmth, moving to gauge mental energy, the bedroom interface translates feelings into data. This is where subjective states become design input, shaping how the layer between you and the world will respond. The bedroom is not just a starting point; it is an act of authorship, letting you design your own comfort and agency before the environment begins to speak.

Function

Captures the body's internal state—readiness, warmth, energy, and visibility intent—through interactive gestures like cursor movement, press-and-hold, and proximity.

Narrative

The mirror is about self-awareness before engagement. It asks: How do you feel in this moment? By translating these subtle internal signals into data, the system allows the user to co-author their experience for the day. It's an intimate starting point—designing from the inside out.

Function

Displays environmental conditions and route planning through live geospatial data (Mapbox + weather APIs). Visual overlays reveal sunlight intensity, wind flow, and crowd density.

Narrative

The window is about perspective. It frames the world outside as an active system—dynamic, unpredictable, and interconnected. The act of looking through the window becomes an exploration: What's out there, and how will it shape your choices? It shifts dressing from aesthetic preference to environmental negotiation.

Function

Offers interactive garment logic settings without explicit labels: sliders and gestures adjust adaptability, ventilation, opacity, and layering in real time.

Narrative

The wardrobe transforms from a static closet into a generative interface. It represents authorship and play: the ability to define not just what to wear, but how that layer will behave across scenarios. It asks: What do you want this garment to do for you today?

Function

Presents the adaptive garment's preview and confirms the system's configuration for the day. A visual summary shows how body inputs and environmental data converge in the design.

Narrative

The door is closure: stepping out, equipped. It carries a sense of transition: from control to release, from intention to movement. It reinforces that dressing is no longer a passive ritual but an adaptive design process responding to both body and world.
